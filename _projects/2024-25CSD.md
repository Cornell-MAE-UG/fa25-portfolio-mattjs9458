As a member of Cornell Seismic Design, I was heavily involved in the design, construction, testing, and analysis of a cable dampening system for the 5-ft tall balsa wood structure. In order to reduce the amplitude and velocity of our building's oscillations, we implemented cross-body cables that on each face of the building that went into tension, pulling the building back to its equilibrium position when the building was shaken under a simulated earthquake. 

Besides the springs and the cables, all of the damper's parts were designed and manufactured using Autodesk Fusion 360. These models were then 3D printed and implemented into the building. I conducted all of the Fusion modeling, and reders of the design can be found in the attached damper proposal. The design, testing, and analysis process was documented in a damper proposal sent to the Earthquake Engineering Research Institute for review and approval in our competition.
